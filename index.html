Introduction

Hello, my name is Phoenix. I'm a college student interested in data science. I am currently taking a data science course where we use different software to analyze data. For my first project, I worked with my group to analyze a data set containing information about the first six generations of Pokemon. I really liked working on this project, so I hope that you enjoy reading about it! :D

Questions

  • Question 1

    What is the relationship between a Pokémon's generation and total stats? Are newer generations stronger than older ones, or has the game stayed balanced over time?

  • Question 2

    Are Pokémon with the highest total stats always the ones with the highest attack stat? Or do Pokémon tend to be balanced across all stats?

  • Question 3

    Are legendary Pokémon actually superior to non-legendary Pokémon across all stats, or do they only dominate in certain areas? Where exactly does the "legendary advantage" show up?

Findings

We found that the different generations are balanced over time and that Pokemon with the highest attack stat do tend to have a higher stat overall. Our analysis also showed that there is a difference between the average total stat of a legendary Pokemon and non-legendary Pokemon, however the differences are not always vast. This means that a player with a team of non-legendary Pokemon would potentially be able to defeat a legendary Pokemon.
